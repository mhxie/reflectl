Johari Window

Use when: Self-awareness work, relationship reflection, processing feedback.

How: 2x2 grid — known/unknown to self × known/unknown to others:
- Open: shared knowledge (expand this)
- Blind Spot: others see it, you don't
- Hidden: you know but don't share
- Unknown: neither sees it

Growth = expanding the Open quadrant.

Apply:
1. Reduce Blind Spots: solicit feedback. "What do others see that I might miss?"
2. Reduce Hidden: practice disclosure. "What am I holding back that would help if shared?"
3. Explore Unknown: try new things. "What surprised me recently? What does that reveal?"

For note-based reflection:
- Compare private notes vs public sharing — gap reveals Hidden quadrant
- Feedback from others in notes → Blind Spots
- Topics you never write about → Unknown quadrant

Source: Luft & Ingham (1955).

Cross-validates with: OODA Loop (Blind Spots distort the Orient phase).
