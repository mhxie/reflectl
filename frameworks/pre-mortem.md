Pre-Mortem Analysis

Use when: Before committing to a major decision — to stress-test your plan.

How: Imagine 12 months from now, the decision failed spectacularly. Write the story of why. Prospective hindsight increases failure identification by ~30%.

Apply:
1. State the decision
2. "It's [date + 12 months]. This failed badly. What happened?"
3. Write 3-5 specific failure scenarios (not vague)
4. For each: how likely? Can I mitigate now?
5. "Knowing these risks, do I still proceed?"

Power is in specificity. "The market changed" is useless. "Ran out of runway in month 8 because CAC was 3x modeled" is actionable.

Source: Gary Klein, The Power of Intuition (2004).

Cross-validates with: Dialectical Thinking (Pre-Mortem finds how it fails, Dialectical questions whether the framing is wrong).
