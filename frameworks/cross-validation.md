# Cross-Validation Guide

Apply two frameworks from different categories to the same question. The power is in the tension between perspectives.

## Primary Pairings

| Core Question | Framework A | Framework B | What the tension reveals |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------------------|
| "What should I pursue?" | Ikigai | Regret Minimization | Ikigai maps fit; Regret tests conviction |
| "Why am I stuck?" | Immunity to Change | Theory of Constraints | Immunity finds internal blocks; TOC finds system blocks |
| "Is this the right decision?" | Pre-Mortem | Dialectical Thinking | Pre-Mortem stress-tests; Dialectical reframes |
| "Am I spending time well?" | Eisenhower Matrix | Double-Loop Learning | Eisenhower prioritizes; Double-Loop questions the goals |
| "What am I missing?" | Johari Window | OODA Loop | Johari reveals blind spots; OODA reveals stale orientation |

## Extended Pairings (New Frameworks)

| Core Question | Framework A | Framework B | What the tension reveals |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|------------------------|
| "Is this worth building?" | First Principles | Inversion | First Principles builds up; Inversion tears down |
| "What happens next?" | Second-Order Thinking | Pre-Mortem | Second-Order traces cascades; Pre-Mortem imagines failure |
| "Am I out of my depth?" | Circle of Competence | Growth Mindset | Circle says "know your limits"; Growth says "limits are movable" |
| "What kind of problem is this?" | Cynefin | Five Whys | Cynefin classifies the domain; Five Whys drills to root cause |
| "What's the real need?" | Jobs to Be Done | Ikigai | JTBD asks why this goal; Ikigai asks if it fits your life |
| "Where should I invest?" | Wardley Mapping | Pareto Principle | Wardley maps evolution; Pareto identifies highest leverage |
| "How do I face this?" | Stoic Reflection | Map of Meaning | Stoic focuses on control; Meaning focuses on purpose |
| "Should I specialize or broaden?" | Circle of Competence | Wardley Mapping | Circle maps current depth; Wardley maps where the market is going |
| "Is my effort well-directed?" | Pareto Principle | Theory of Constraints | Pareto finds leverage; TOC finds bottleneck |
| "Am I growing or just busy?" | Growth Mindset | Double-Loop Learning | Growth asks "am I learning?"; Double-Loop asks "am I learning the right thing?" |

## Protocol

1. **Apply Framework A.** Write down its conclusion independently.
2. **Apply Framework B.** Don't let A bias B — approach fresh.
3. **Compare:**

| Result | Interpretation | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Agreement** | High confidence | Proceed with conviction |
| **Partial overlap** | Overlapping part is solid | Focus on the overlap; investigate the divergence |
| **Disagreement** | Gold | Ask: "What assumption does each make that the other doesn't?" The gap is where the real insight lives |

4. **Synthesize:** The best insight usually comes from the tension, not from either framework alone.

## When NOT to Cross-Validate

- One framework clearly fits and the situation is simple
- You're over-analyzing to avoid action (if 2+ frameworks applied, stop and act)
- The user needs emotional support, not analytical rigor
- The situation is chaotic (Cynefin: act first, analyze later)
