Theory of Constraints

Use when: Effort isn't producing proportional results. Goal progress reviews.

How: Identify the single bottleneck that limits throughput of the entire system. Subordinate everything else to it. In personal terms: what one thing, if resolved, would unblock the most progress?

Apply:
1. List active goals and their blockers
2. "If I could only fix ONE blocker, which would unblock the most progress across all goals?"
3. That's your constraint. Options:
   - Exploit: get more out of it without adding resources
   - Elevate: invest more to break through
   - Subordinate: stop optimizing everything else, redirect energy here

Common trap: optimizing non-bottleneck activities (reading more books when the constraint is taking action on what you already know).

Source: Goldratt, The Goal (1984).

Cross-validates with: Immunity to Change (TOC finds systemic bottleneck, ITC finds psychological one).
