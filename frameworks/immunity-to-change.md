Immunity to Change

Use when: You keep failing to change a behavior despite genuine motivation — the knowing-doing gap.

How: Map four columns:
1. Stated commitment ("I want to X")
2. What you're doing instead ("But I keep doing Y")
3. Hidden competing commitment ("Because I'm also protecting Z")
4. Big assumption ("I assume that if I stop protecting Z, then...")

Reveals that resistance isn't about willpower — it's an internal immune system protecting deeply held beliefs.

Apply:
1. "What do you genuinely want to change?"
2. "What are you doing that works against this?"
3. "If you imagine doing the opposite, what feels scary?" — reveals the competing commitment
4. "What assumption would have to be true for that fear to be justified?"
5. "Is that assumption actually true? How could you test it?"

Source: Kegan & Lahey, Harvard. Immunity to Change (2009).

Cross-validates with: Theory of Constraints (ITC finds psychological bottleneck, TOC finds systemic one).
