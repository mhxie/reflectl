Dialectical Thinking

Use when: Stuck in binary thinking or inner conflict — "Should I stay or go?"

How: Thesis → Antithesis → Synthesis.
1. State your position (thesis)
2. Articulate the strongest opposing position with genuine empathy (antithesis)
3. Find a resolution that transcends the contradiction (synthesis)

This is NOT compromise. It's finding a higher-order frame.

Example:
- Thesis: "Take the safe job for stability"
- Antithesis: "Pursue the risky startup for fulfillment"
- Synthesis: "Take safe job for 18 months to build runway, then leap from strength"

The synthesis often reframes timeline, scope, or definition in a way neither side considered.

Source: Hegel (19th century). Operationalized in DBT by Linehan (1993).

Cross-validates with: Pre-Mortem (Dialectical questions the frame, Pre-Mortem stress-tests the chosen path).
