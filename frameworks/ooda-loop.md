OODA Loop

Use when: Fast-moving situations, career pivots, navigating ambiguity.

How: Observe → Orient → Decide → Act → repeat. Speed of cycling > perfection of any step. The Orient phase is critical — where biases live and breakthroughs happen.

Apply:
1. Observe: "What am I actually seeing right now?" (separate signal from noise)
2. Orient: "How does this change my mental model?" — the hard step
   - What cultural assumptions am I bringing?
   - What prior experience is coloring my interpretation?
   - What new understanding emerges from analysis/synthesis?
3. Decide: "Given this updated model, what should I do?"
4. Act: execute, then feed results back into Observe

Most people get stuck at Orient — they observe but interpret through stale models. Ask: "What would I think if I had no prior beliefs about this?"

Source: Col. John Boyd, USAF (1976).

Cross-validates with: Johari Window (Blind Spots distort Orient phase).
