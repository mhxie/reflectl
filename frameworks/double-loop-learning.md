Double-Loop Learning

Use when: Recurring problems, repeated failures — suspect you're solving the wrong problem.

How:
- Single-loop: adjusts actions to achieve existing goals (thermostat: too cold → turn up heat)
- Double-loop: questions the goals themselves (why is the thermostat set to this temperature?)

Apply:
1. "What have I been trying to do about this?"
2. "How many times have I tried variations of the same approach?"
3. "What goal or assumption am I taking for granted?"
4. "What if that goal or assumption is wrong?"

Example:
- Single-loop: "How do I force myself to wake at 6am?"
- Double-loop: "Why do I believe early rising is necessary? What if I'm a night owl who does best work at 11pm?"

Sign you need double-loop: you've tried 3+ approaches to the same problem and none stuck.

Source: Argyris & Schön, Organizational Learning (1978).

Cross-validates with: Eisenhower Matrix (Double-Loop questions priorities, Eisenhower re-sorts after reframing).
