Eisenhower Matrix

Use when: Overwhelmed, procrastinating on meaningful work, need to prioritize.

How: Classify into four quadrants:
- Q1 Urgent+Important: do now
- Q2 Important+Not Urgent: schedule — THIS IS WHERE GROWTH LIVES
- Q3 Urgent+Not Important: delegate/minimize
- Q4 Neither: eliminate

Key insight: personal growth, relationships, and strategic thinking live in Q2, which gets systematically neglected because Q1 and Q3 consume all attention.

Apply:
1. List everything competing for your time this week
2. Classify each into a quadrant
3. How much time goes to Q2 vs Q1/Q3?
4. Hard question: "Which Q1 items are actually Q3 in disguise?" (feels urgent but isn't important)

Source: Covey, 7 Habits (1989).

Cross-validates with: Double-Loop Learning (Eisenhower sorts tasks, Double-Loop questions whether the categories are right).
